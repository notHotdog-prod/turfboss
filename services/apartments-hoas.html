The Smart Choice for Community Properties

For property managers, HOA boards, and apartment complex owners, artificial turf isn't just aesthetically appealing — it's a transformational financial decision. The average Ocean County HOA with common grass areas spends $8,000–$15,000 or more per year on landscaping services, irrigation, and water costs. Artificial turf eliminates the vast majority of that ongoing expense.

Imagine converting your community's common lawn areas to premium artificial turf: no more weekly mowing crews, no irrigation system maintenance, no spring and fall reseedings, no drought-related emergencies. Just consistent, beautiful green spaces that residents enjoy year-round.

FAQ

Common Questions

How does the cost compare to our current landscaping contract?+

Most HOA common areas pay back the installation cost within 2–4 years through eliminated service contracts and water costs. We can provide a detailed ROI analysis for your community.

Can you phase the installation over multiple years?+

Yes. Many HOAs prefer to phase large installations over 2–3 years to spread capital costs. We can develop a phased plan that fits your budget cycle.

How do we get HOA board approval?+

We provide complete product documentation, installation specifications, warranty information, and reference photos to help you present the proposal to your HOA board. We've supported many successful NJ HOA approvals.

Is there a minimum project size?+

We welcome all project sizes but offer the best pricing on community-scale installations of 5,000+ sq ft. Contact us to discuss your community's specific needs.
