Artificial Turf Maintenance Guide

Good news: artificial turf requires 95% less maintenance than natural grass. Here's everything you need to know.

The Good News

Lawn Care Is Now a Thing of the Past

When you choose artificial turf, you're choosing freedom. No more weekly mowing. No irrigation system to maintain. No fertilizer schedules. No brown patches to reseed. Your turf stays lush and green through New Jersey winters, summer heat waves, and everything in between.

That said, a small amount of simple maintenance will keep your turf looking its best for 15–25 years. This guide covers everything you need to know.

Maintenance Tasks

Simple Steps to Keep Your Turf Perfect

🧹 Brushing & Raking (Monthly or After Heavy Use)+

Periodically brushing your artificial turf keeps the fibers standing upright and maintains that natural, lush appearance. Use a stiff-bristled brush or a plastic lawn rake — never metal tines, which can damage the turf backing. Brush against the grain (opposite the direction the blades naturally lay) to revive flattened areas. Focus on high-traffic zones like pathways and play areas. For large areas, a power broom makes the job much faster.

Frequency: Monthly, or more often in high-traffic zones. After any large gathering or heavy use event.

🚿 Rinsing & Cleaning (As Needed)+

Light hosing removes dust, pollen, and airborne debris that can accumulate on the surface. During NJ's allergy season (spring and fall), a gentle rinse once or twice a week keeps the turf clean and fresh. For deeper cleaning, use a mild soap solution and a soft brush, then rinse thoroughly. Avoid harsh chemicals, bleach, or solvents — these can damage the turf fibers and infill.

Safe cleaners: Mild dish soap, white vinegar diluted 1:1 with water, enzymatic pet odor cleaners (for pet areas). Avoid: Bleach, solvents, acetone, or harsh degreasers.

🍂 Debris Removal (Weekly or As Needed)+

Leaves, twigs, and other natural debris should be removed regularly to prevent organic material from breaking down into the infill, which can cause odors and support weed growth at the edges. Use a leaf blower for efficient removal — it works perfectly on artificial turf without damaging fibers. A plastic rake is also effective for larger debris. NJ's fall season means more frequent attention during October and November.

🐾 Pet Waste Removal (Daily for Solid Waste)+

Remove solid pet waste daily using a biodegradable bag. Rinse the area with a garden hose to dilute and wash away liquid waste into the drainage system below. For odor control in pet areas, apply an enzymatic cleaner (like Nature's Miracle or a similar product) once a week, or more frequently in warm weather. Our antimicrobial turf infill options significantly reduce odor between cleanings. Avoid ammonia-based cleaners as they can attract pets back to the same spots.

🔍 Inspecting Seams & Edges (Monthly)+

Once a month, walk your turf perimeter and check for any lifting edges or visible seams. In NJ's freeze-thaw cycles, ground movement can occasionally cause minor lifting at the edges. Catching this early prevents larger issues. If you notice any seam separation or edge lifting, contact Turf Boss NJ promptly — small repairs done quickly prevent larger problems. Our installations include a workmanship warranty, so call us first if you see any issues.

❄️ Winter & Seasonal Care in NJ+

One of the best things about artificial turf in New Jersey is how little you have to do in winter. Your turf handles NJ winters beautifully on its own. If snow accumulates, you can leave it to melt naturally (preferred) or gently remove it with a plastic snow shovel — never metal-edged tools. Avoid using rock salt or chemical ice melt directly on the turf surface as it can damage fibers and backing. Sand-based ice melt is safer if needed at edges.

In spring, give the turf a good rinse and brushing to refresh it for the new season. Check edges and seams after the freeze-thaw season.
